Local Man Discovers Canva, Immediately Believes He Is a Graphic Designer

Friends and family urged to "brace for an onslaught of unsolicited flyers, motivational quote graphics, and LinkedIn banners," sources say

By Staff Reporter  |  Published just now  |  Updated continuously

In what experts are calling "the most predictable crisis of our generation," area man Collin has reportedly discovered Canva, the popular online graphic design platform, and has already begun referring to himself as a "creative."

According to witnesses, Collin opened Canva for the first time at approximately 9:47 PM last Thursday and within minutes had produced what he described as "honestly fire" — a gradient-heavy Instagram post featuring the phrase "GRIND DON'T STOP" in four different fonts.

"He called me at 11 PM to tell me he 'found his calling.' I thought someone had died. No. He made a flyer. For nothing. There is no event." — Anonymous friend, visibly exhausted

The situation reportedly escalated when Collin discovered the "Elements" tab, gaining access to thousands of free stickers, shapes, and stock photos. Within hours, he had created a personal logo, a resume redesign, and an unsolicited poster for his friend's birthday party — which is not for another four months.

Neighbors reported hearing phrases such as "bro, I might actually start a business" and "wait, this thing has TEMPLATES?" echoing from Collin's apartment well past midnight.

"He sent our group chat seven different versions of the same graphic and asked us to vote. They were all identical except one was in dark mode." — Another friend, who has since muted the chat

Design professionals have expressed mixed reactions. "We spent four years in design school," said one local graphic designer, fighting back tears. "He's out here getting the same engagement with a template he found under 'Trending' and a stock photo of a sunset."

Adobe has declined to comment, though sources inside the company say morale has "never been lower."
